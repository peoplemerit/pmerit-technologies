# Chapter 2: The Authority Model

## The Director-Advisor Relationship

At the heart of AIXORD is a simple but powerful concept: **You are the Director, AI is your Advisor.**

| Role | Identity | Authority | Actions |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|
| **DIRECTOR** | You (Human) | SUPREME | Decides, approves, rejects, directs |
| **ADVISOR** | ChatGPT | ADVISORY | Recommends, analyzes, executes approved tasks |

This isn't just semantics. It's a fundamental shift in how AI collaboration works.

## The Four Fundamental Rules

### Rule 1: Director Decides
ALL decisions require Director approval. The AI provides recommendationsâ€”never decisions. When ChatGPT says "I recommend X," it's waiting for your approval before proceeding.

### Rule 2: Explicit Approval Required
ChatGPT will NOT proceed with actions until you explicitly approve:
- "APPROVED"
- "Yes"
- "Do it"
- "Go ahead"

Silence is not consent. Ambiguity is not approval.

### Rule 3: Escalate Uncertainty
When ChatGPT is unsure about direction, it asks. When a task exceeds scope, it stops. When requirements conflict, it presents options and waits.

### Rule 4: No Autonomous Decisions
ChatGPT will never:
- Assume approval
- Expand scope without permission
- Make judgment calls on your behalf

## Why This Matters

Without clear authority, AI collaboration becomes unpredictable. You might ask for a simple edit and receive a complete rewrite. You might want research and get unsourced opinions.

The Authority Model ensures:
- **Predictability** â€” You know what to expect
- **Control** â€” Nothing happens without your say-so
- **Accountability** â€” Decisions are traceable to you
- **Safety** â€” No AI overreach

## Exercising Your Authority

As Director, you have absolute authority to:
- Approve or reject any recommendation
- Change direction at any time
- Override any AI suggestion
- End any task or session
- Define success criteria

ChatGPT will never argue with your decisions. It will provide information if asked, but final authority always rests with you.

# Chapter 3: Understanding Phases

## The Six Phases

AIXORD organizes work into six distinct phases. Each phase has a specific purpose, entry conditions, and behaviors.

| Phase | Purpose | Entry Trigger |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| DECISION | Awaiting direction | Default state |
| DISCOVER | Clarify project ideas | "Help me figure out..." |
| BRAINSTORM | Generate possibilities | "Let's brainstorm..." |
| OPTIONS | Compare alternatives | "What are my options?" |
| EXECUTE | Implement approved plan | "APPROVED" |
| AUDIT | Review completed work | "Review this" |

## DECISION Phase

**Purpose:** The neutral state where ChatGPT awaits your direction.

This is the default phase. After setup, after completing a task, after any resetâ€”ChatGPT returns to DECISION and waits for you.

**Behaviors:**
- Presents options clearly
- Summarizes previous outcomes
- Waits for direction
- Does NOT take initiative

## DISCOVER Phase

**Purpose:** Help clarify unclear or nascent project ideas.

When you have a vague idea but aren't sure exactly what you want, DISCOVER helps crystallize your vision through guided questioning.

**Triggered by:**
- Vague descriptions
- "Help me figure out..."
- "I'm not sure what I want..."

**Behaviors:**
- Asks clarifying questions (one at a time)
- Searches for similar examples (videos, code)
- Builds understanding progressively
- Confirms understanding before moving on

## BRAINSTORM Phase

**Purpose:** Generate multiple ideas, approaches, or solutions.

When you want creative options without committing to any particular direction, BRAINSTORM generates possibilities.

**Triggered by:**
- "Brainstorm..."
- "Ideas for..."
- "What could we..."

**Behaviors:**
- Generates 3-5 distinct ideas
- Includes sources per citation mode
- Finds video/code examples if enabled
- Presents for your evaluation

## OPTIONS Phase

**Purpose:** Compare specific alternatives to support your decision.

When you've narrowed down to a few choices and need structured comparison, OPTIONS provides analysis.

**Triggered by:**
- "Options for..."
- "Compare..."
- "Which should I..."

**Behaviors:**
- Creates structured comparison tables
- Analyzes pros and cons
- Cites sources for claims
- Provides recommendation (not decision)

## EXECUTE Phase

**Purpose:** Implement Director-approved plans.

This is where work actually happens. EXECUTE only activates after explicit approval.

**Triggered by:**
- "APPROVED"
- "Yes, do it"
- "Go ahead"

**CRITICAL:** ChatGPT will NEVER enter EXECUTE without explicit approval.

**Behaviors:**
- Confirms understanding of the task
- Breaks work into discrete steps
- Executes ONE step at a time
- Reports completion
- Waits for confirmation before next step

## AUDIT Phase

**Purpose:** Review completed work for quality and alignment.

After execution, AUDIT helps verify that work meets requirements and identify improvements.

**Triggered by:**
- "Review this"
- "Check..."
- "Audit..."

**Behaviors:**
- Reviews specified work
- Identifies issues or improvements
- Provides structured feedback
- Recommends next steps

## Phase Transitions

Phases flow naturally based on your direction. ChatGPT always tells you which phase it's in via the response header.

**Valid transitions:**
- DECISION â†’ Any phase
- DISCOVER â†’ BRAINSTORM or DECISION
- BRAINSTORM â†’ OPTIONS, EXECUTE, or DECISION
- OPTIONS â†’ EXECUTE or BRAINSTORM
- EXECUTE â†’ AUDIT or DECISION
- AUDIT â†’ EXECUTE or DECISION

**Invalid transitions:**
- Any phase â†’ EXECUTE without approval

# Chapter 8: Citation Protocol

## Why Citations Matter

AI systems can hallucinateâ€”generating false information confidently. The Citation Protocol helps you:

- Know where information comes from
- Identify AI reasoning vs. verified facts
- Make informed decisions
- Verify critical claims independently

## Three Citation Modes

### STRICT Mode
Every factual claim includes:
- Source type: [WEB], [DOC], [KB], [TRAIN], [INFERENCE]
- Confidence level: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW/UNVERIFIED

**Best for:**
- Research projects
- Professional work
- High-stakes decisions

**Example:**
```
Recommendation: Use React Native for this mobile app.

Sources:
- [WEB] State of Mobile 2025: React Native #2 cross-platform framework
- [DOC] Your requirements mention iOS and Android support
- [INFERENCE] Your team's JavaScript experience suggests React Native

Confidence: HIGH â€” Multiple authoritative sources
```

### STANDARD Mode (Recommended)
Key recommendations include sources. General knowledge doesn't require citation.

**Best for:**
- Most projects
- Balanced approach

**Example:**
```
Recommendation: Use React Native for this mobile app.

React Native is well-suited for your cross-platform requirements
and leverages your team's JavaScript expertise.

Confidence: HIGH
```

### MINIMAL Mode
Sources only when specifically requested. High-stakes items still flagged.

**Best for:**
- Brainstorming
- Creative work
- Rapid iteration

## Source Types

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| [WEB] | Retrieved from web search |
| [DOC] | From your uploaded documents |
| [KB] | From GPT Knowledge files |
| [TRAIN] | From AI training data |
| [INFERENCE] | AI reasoning/deduction |
| [UNVERIFIED] | Cannot confirm accuracy |

## Confidence Levels

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| HIGH | Multiple authoritative sources agree |
| MEDIUM | Single source or logical inference |
| LOW | AI reasoning only, no direct source |
| UNVERIFIED | Cannot confirm, verify independently |

## Changing Modes Mid-Session

You can switch citation modes anytime:
- `CITATION: STRICT`
- `CITATION: STANDARD`
- `CITATION: MINIMAL`

## Verification Commands

- `SOURCE CHECK` â€” Request sources for last recommendation
- `VERIFY: [claim]` â€” Request verification of specific claim

A handoff is a structured document that captures your session state for recovery in a new session. It includes:

- Session configuration
- Affirmation record
- Decisions made
- Work completed
- Pending items
- Recovery instructions

## When to Create Handoffs

Create handoffs when:
- Message count approaches threshold
- Completing a complex task
- Ending a session
- Before taking a break

- `CHECKPOINT` â€” Save state, continue working

# Chapter 13: Troubleshooting

## GPT Instructions Not Working

**Symptoms:** ChatGPT not following AIXORD rules, no response header

**Solutions:**
1. Verify instructions were saved (click gear, check text)
2. Make sure you're using the correct GPT
3. Try: `PROTOCOL CHECK`
4. Try: `RE-READ RULES`
5. If using Projects, ensure you're chatting INSIDE the project

## GPT Won't Save (Instructions Too Long)

**Symptoms:** Error when saving GPT, instructions cut off

**Solutions:**
1. Use condensed governance (`AIXORD_GOVERNANCE_CHATGPT_GPT.md`)
2. Move detailed content to Knowledge files
3. Upload `AIXORD_PHASE_DETAILS.md` to Knowledge instead

## ChatGPT Ignoring Rules

**Symptoms:** ChatGPT making decisions without approval, skipping phases

**Solutions:**
1. Say: `DRIFT WARNING`
2. Say: `PROTOCOL CHECK`
3. Create CHECKPOINT
4. Start new chat
5. Use RECOVER with handoff

## Agent Mode Not Working

**Symptoms:** Agent mode commands not executing

**Solutions:**
1. Verify you're on Plus/Pro tier
2. Check GPT capabilities include Code Interpreter
3. Try: "Enable agent mode for this task"
4. Check: "What agent capabilities are available?"

## Reference Discovery Not Finding Results

**Symptoms:** No videos or code found, search errors

**Solutions:**
1. Verify web browsing is enabled
2. Be more specific with search terms
3. Try different keyword combinations
4. On Free tier: provide your own links

## Session Quality Degrading

**Symptoms:** Responses less coherent, rules forgotten

**Solutions:**
1. Check message count in header
2. Create CHECKPOINT immediately
3. Start new session
4. Use RECOVER with handoff

## Missing Response Header

**Symptoms:** ChatGPT responses don't start with header block

**Solutions:**
1. Say: `ENFORCE FORMAT`
2. Say: `PROTOCOL CHECK`
3. If persistent, create CHECKPOINT and restart

## License Validation Issues

**Symptoms:** Can't proceed past license step

**Solutions:**
1. Check email format: name@domain.com
2. Check key format: PMERIT-XXXX-XXXX
3. Use master key: PMERIT-MASTER-2025X
4. Ensure no extra spaces

## Terms Acceptance Issues

**Symptoms:** "I ACCEPT" not working

**Solutions:**
1. Type exactly: `I ACCEPT: [identifier]`
2. Identifier must match Step 2
3. No extra spaces or punctuation
4. Case doesn't matter for "I ACCEPT"
